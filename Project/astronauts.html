Legendary Astronauts

The pioneers who made history in space exploration

Yuri Gagarin

First Human in Space

On April 12, 1961, Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin became the first human to journey into outer space, orbiting Earth once in the Vostok 1 spacecraft. His historic 108-minute flight opened the door to human space exploration.

  • Mission: Vostok 1
  • Date: April 12, 1961
  • Duration: 108 minutes
  • Country: Soviet Union
Pioneer
Neil Armstrong

First Person on the Moon

As commander of Apollo 11, Neil Armstrong became the first person to set foot on the Moon on July 20, 1969, famously declaring it "one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."

  • Mission: Apollo 11
  • Date: July 20, 1969
  • Duration: 8 days
  • Country: United States
Moonwalker
Sally Ride

First American Woman in Space

Sally Ride made history on June 18, 1983, as the first American woman to fly in space aboard the Space Shuttle Challenger. She later flew on STS-41-G in 1984 and became an advocate for science education.

  • Mission: STS-7
  • Date: June 18, 1983
  • Flights: 2
  • Country: United States
Trailblazer
Buzz Aldrin

Second Person on the Moon

Lunar Module Pilot on Apollo 11, Buzz Aldrin followed Neil Armstrong onto the Moon's surface, spending over 21 hours on the lunar surface and conducting crucial experiments.

  • Mission: Apollo 11
  • Date: July 20, 1969
  • Moonwalks: 1
  • Country: United States
Moonwalker
Valentina Tereshkova

First Woman in Space

On June 16, 1963, Valentina Tereshkova became the first woman to fly in space aboard Vostok 6. She orbited Earth 48 times over three days, logging more flight time than all American astronauts combined at that point.

  • Mission: Vostok 6
  • Date: June 16, 1963
  • Orbits: 48
  • Country: Soviet Union
Pioneer
Chris Hadfield

ISS Commander & Space Educator

Canadian astronaut who served as commander of the International Space Station and became famous for his engaging social media presence, including performing "Space Oddity" in orbit.

  • Missions: 3 flights
  • ISS Command: Expedition 35
  • Spacewalks: 2
  • Country: Canada
Educator

Historic Space Missions

Groundbreaking missions that changed our understanding of space

Apollo 11 - Moon Landing

July 16-24, 1969

The first crewed mission to land on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin spent 21 hours on the lunar surface while Michael Collins orbited above.

Moonwalkers: 2 Historic
Apollo 13 - Survival Mission

April 11-17, 1970

Intended as the third lunar landing, Apollo 13 became a dramatic rescue mission after an oxygen tank explosion. The crew safely returned to Earth through incredible teamwork.

Successful Return Crisis
Voyager Program

Launched 1977

Twin spacecraft that explored Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Voyager 1 is now the most distant human-made object, in interstellar space.

Ongoing Interstellar
ISS Assembly & Operations

1998 - Present

The largest space station ever built, a collaborative project of 15 nations. Has been continuously occupied since November 2000.

Operational International
Mars Rover Missions

1997 - Present

Series of robotic rovers exploring Mars, including Sojourner, Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance. Searching for signs of past life and studying Martian geology.

Mars Active
Commercial Crew Program

2020 - Present

SpaceX's Crew Dragon restored American capability to launch astronauts from US soil, marking the beginning of commercial human spaceflight.

Commercial Operational

Notable Spacewalks

Venturing outside the spacecraft into the void

First Spacewalk

Alexei Leonov - March 18, 1965

Soviet cosmonaut Alexei Leonov performed the first-ever spacewalk, spending 12 minutes outside the Voskhod 2 spacecraft. His suit inflated dangerously, making re-entry nearly impossible.

Historic First
First American Spacewalk

Ed White - June 3, 1965

Ed White became the first American to walk in space during Gemini 4, spending 23 minutes floating above Earth. He was so thrilled that ground control had to order him back inside.

Gemini 4
First All-Female Spacewalk

Christina Koch & Jessica Meir - October 2019

NASA astronauts Christina Koch and Jessica Meir conducted the first all-female spacewalk outside the ISS, replacing a faulty battery charge-discharge unit.
