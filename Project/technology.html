Space Technology

Rockets & Launch Vehicles

Powerful machines that propel humanity beyond Earth

SpaceX Falcon Heavy

The world's most powerful operational rocket, capable of lifting over 63 metric tons to low Earth orbit. Features reusable boosters that land vertically after launch.

  • Payload to LEO: 63,800 kg
  • Height: 70 meters
  • Reusable boosters
Operational Reusable
NASA Space Launch System

NASA's super heavy-lift launch vehicle designed for deep space missions, including returning humans to the Moon through the Artemis program.

  • Payload to LEO: 95,000 kg
  • Height: 98 meters
  • Moon missions
Operational Heavy Lift
SpaceX Starship

The largest and most powerful rocket ever built, fully reusable and designed to carry up to 100 passengers to Mars and beyond.

  • Payload to LEO: 100-150 tons
  • Height: 120 meters
  • Fully reusable
In Development Reusable
Ariane 6

Europe's new generation launch vehicle, designed for versatility and cost-effectiveness, serving both commercial and government missions.

  • Payload to GTO: 11,500 kg
  • Height: 63 meters
  • Multiple configurations
Operational
Long March 5

China's largest and most powerful rocket, essential for the Chinese space station construction and lunar exploration programs.

  • Payload to LEO: 25,000 kg
  • Height: 57 meters
  • Heavy lift capability
Operational
Blue Origin New Glenn

A heavy-lift orbital launch vehicle with a reusable first stage, designed to compete in the commercial launch market.

  • Payload to LEO: 45,000 kg
  • Height: 95 meters
  • Reusable first stage
In Development Reusable

Satellites & Spacecraft

Orbiting platforms that connect, observe, and explore

International Space Station

A habitable artificial satellite serving as a microgravity laboratory, hosting astronauts from around the world continuously since 2000.

  • Orbit: 400 km altitude
  • Speed: 28,000 km/h
  • Mass: 420,000 kg
  • Crew: 7 astronauts
Operational
GPS Satellite Network

A constellation of 31 satellites providing global positioning, navigation, and timing services to billions of users worldwide.

  • Orbit: 20,200 km altitude
  • Satellites: 31 active
  • Coverage: Global
  • Accuracy: Within 5 meters
Operational
Weather Satellites

Geostationary and polar-orbiting satellites monitoring Earth's atmosphere, providing crucial data for weather forecasting and climate research.

  • Types: Geostationary & Polar
  • Orbit: 36,000 km / 850 km
  • Coverage: Global
  • Updates: Continuous
Operational
Starlink Constellation

A mega-constellation of thousands of satellites providing high-speed internet connectivity to remote and underserved areas globally.

  • Orbit: 550 km altitude
  • Satellites: 5,000+ active
  • Coverage: Global
  • Speed: Up to 200 Mbps
Operational

Space Telescopes

Eyes in the sky revealing the secrets of the universe

Hubble Space Telescope

Launched in 1990, Hubble has revolutionized astronomy with stunning images and groundbreaking discoveries about the universe's age, expansion, and composition.

  • Launch: 1990
  • Mirror: 2.4 meters
  • Orbit: 540 km
  • Wavelengths: UV to Near-IR
Operational
James Webb Space Telescope

The most powerful space telescope ever built, observing the universe in infrared to see the first galaxies and study exoplanet atmospheres.

  • Launch: 2021
  • Mirror: 6.5 meters
  • Location: L2 Point
  • Wavelengths: Infrared
Operational Most Powerful
Chandra X-ray Observatory

Launched in 1999, Chandra detects X-ray emissions from hot regions of the universe, including black holes, supernovae, and galaxy clusters.

  • Launch: 1999
  • Mirror: 1.2 meters
  • Orbit: Highly elliptical
  • Wavelengths: X-ray
Operational
Spitzer Space Telescope

An infrared space telescope that operated from 2003 to 2020, studying star formation, exoplanets, and the most distant galaxies in the universe.

  • Operational: 2003-2020
  • Mirror: 0.85 meters
  • Orbit: Earth-trailing
  • Wavelengths: Infrared
Retired
Kepler Space Telescope

Revolutionized exoplanet research by discovering over 2,600 confirmed planets, proving that planets are common throughout our galaxy.

  • Operational: 2009-2018
  • Mirror: 0.95 meters
  • Discoveries: 2,600+ planets
  • Mission: Exoplanet hunting
Retired
Nancy Grace Roman Telescope

An upcoming infrared space telescope with a field of view 100 times larger than Hubble, designed to study dark energy and exoplanets.

  • Launch: ~2027
  • Mirror: 2.4 meters
  • Field of View: 100x Hubble
  • Wavelengths: Infrared
In Development
